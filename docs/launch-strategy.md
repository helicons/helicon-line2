# Helicon Launch Strategy — ORB Framework + Road to €1M

## Overview

Helicon is entering a market with zero dominant infrastructure. Independent trap, drill, and reggaetón artists in Madrid spend hours hunting for studio time through WhatsApp groups and overpay for beats through sketchy marketplaces. The launch strategy treats that friction as the core message — Helicon removes it.

This document applies the ORB Framework (Owned, Rented, Borrowed channels) to the Helicon go-to-market, defines a 5-phase launch sequence, and maps milestones to the €1M revenue goal.

---

## ORB Channel Strategy

### Owned Channels

Owned channels are the only ones Helicon controls entirely. They must be built first because every rented and borrowed effort should funnel back here.

**Email Waitlist**

The waitlist is the pre-launch asset. Its job is not just collecting emails — it is segmenting artists by intent.

- Signup form collects: city (Madrid / another city), primary genre (trap / drill / reggaetón / R&B / other), role (artist / producer / both), and one open field: "What's your biggest problem right now with recording?"
- Use the open-field answers verbatim in copy. If 60% say "studios are always full on weekends," that becomes an ad headline.
- Welcome email delivers: (1) an exclusive Metro Shadows or Helicon Origin beat preview not available anywhere else, (2) a clear description of early access perks, (3) a one-question survey to deepen segmentation.
- Weekly email cadence during pre-launch: one email per week, always leading with something useful (a beat breakdown, a studio session reel, a tip about mixing trap vocals). Never purely promotional.
- Referral mechanic baked into the waitlist: "Move up the list — share your link, get priority access." This converts the list itself into a distribution channel.
- Target: 2,000 waitlist signups before Early Access launch.

**Discord Community — "Helicon Studio"**

Discord serves a purpose email cannot: it creates social proof and peer accountability among artists.

- Channels: `#beats-feedback` (artists share work-in-progress, producers react), `#studio-sessions` (behind-the-scenes clips from Neon Room, The Vault, 808 Suite), `#collab-find` (Madrid artists looking for features), `#gear-talk`, `#announcements`.
- Producers from the Helicon roster (starting with the most accessible profiles) appear in Discord weekly for live beat feedback sessions. This is the hook that makes membership worth having.
- Milestone unlocks: at 500 members, drop a free unreleased beat pack. At 1,000, host a live cypher in The Vault streamed on TikTok.
- Discord is also the fastest feedback loop for product decisions. Post mockups, get reactions within hours.
- Target: 500 Discord members by end of Beta phase.

### Rented Channels

Rented channels (paid/algorithmic) are used to generate reach fast. The investment is time and creative energy, not primarily money at launch.

**TikTok — Primary Rented Channel**

TikTok's algorithm rewards content that creates a loop: watch, share, come back. Helicon has two native content types that do this well.

Content formats:
1. **Beat previews** — 15-30 second clips of upcoming marketplace beats (Metro Shadows, Helicon Origin) with artist reaction in split-screen. Caption: "Este beat tiene 2.3M plays. 29€. Nadie sabe quién lo hizo." Tags: #trap #drill #productoresmadrid #beatsfreetoose (intentional misspelling used by the scene)
2. **Studio session BTS** — Vertical camera footage from inside Neon Room or 808 Suite. Not polished. Genuine. Artist recording a hook, engineer adjusting a mix, the moment a take works. Caption: "Grabando en Madrid a las 2AM. 20€/h. Sin lista de espera."
3. **"What €29 gets you"** series — Screen recording of the beat marketplace: filter by genre, listen to a Helicon Origin beat, show the license options. Demystifies the purchase process for artists who've never bought a license.
4. **Producer face reveals** — Short clips introducing a producer from the roster. "Él hizo el beat de [viral song]. Ahora puedes contratar un beat personalizado."

Posting cadence: 5 posts per week minimum. TikTok rewards volume during the growth phase. Quality threshold is "authentic," not "produced."

KPI: 10,000 TikTok followers before Full Launch. At least 2 videos with >50k views before Early Access opens.

**Instagram Reels**

Instagram Reels serves a slightly older slice of the target audience (22-28) and is where A&R managers and producers browse. Repurpose the best TikTok content here with minor adjustments (longer captions are fine on Instagram).

Priority content for Instagram: producer profiles, finished track reveals from artists who booked through Helicon, aesthetic shots of the studios (Neon Room's LED setup, The Vault's SSL console).

Use Instagram Stories for urgency mechanics: "3 slots left this weekend at 808 Suite" with a swipe-up to book.

KPI: 5,000 Instagram followers before Full Launch. Stories should be posted daily during Early Access.

### Borrowed Channels

Borrowed channels are other people's audiences. The cost is relationship and relevance — never money at the start.

**Spanish Rap YouTubers and Streamers**

The Spanish rap YouTube scene has a clear tier: channels like Portavoz TV, ElXokas (broader but covers music), and niche channels dedicated to Madrid drill and trap. These audiences are Helicon's exact target.

Outreach approach:
- Do not send a press kit. Send a message that feels like one creator reaching out to another.
- Offer: exclusive first look at the Helicon beat marketplace before launch, with a custom referral code they can share with their audience (they earn a cut per conversion — this is the affiliate angle).
- For streamers: offer a sponsored studio session. Stream live from inside The Vault or 808 Suite. The content writes itself.
- Minimum target: 3 YouTubers with 20k+ subscribers covering Helicon before Full Launch.

**Established Madrid Producers**

The 12 producers already on the Helicon roster are the first borrowed channel. Their existing social following and credibility are marketing assets.

- Each producer should announce their Helicon presence through their own channels with a post template Helicon provides but that reads in their voice.
- Coordinate a "producer drop day" where all 12 post on the same day — Instagram, TikTok — announcing their beats are now on Helicon. This creates the impression of a major event.
- Beyond the roster: identify 3-5 established Madrid producers not on Helicon yet. Offer them a guest beat slot (1-2 beats listed, no exclusivity required) in exchange for a post to their audience. This borrowing-before-buying builds credibility fast.

## 5-Phase Launch Plan

### Phase 1 — Internal (Weeks 1-4)

**Who:** Core team + the 12 roster producers.

**Goal:** Stress-test the full booking flow, beat purchase flow, and producer profile experience. Find every broken edge case before any external user sees it.

**Actions:**
- Each producer books a test studio session through the platform and purchases a test beat license.
- Document every point of confusion. Fix before moving on.
- Set up analytics: Mixpanel events for beat play, beat purchase, studio booking initiated, booking completed.
- Record first BTS content inside the studios (do not publish yet — build the content library).
- Write and schedule the first 20 TikTok drafts.

**Exit Criteria:** Zero critical bugs in the booking flow. All producer profiles complete with at least 3 beats listed.

---

### Phase 2 — Alpha (Weeks 5-8)

**Who:** 50-100 hand-picked artists from personal networks of the team and the roster producers.

**Goal:** Validate that the product creates real value before spending on acquisition. Get the first real studio bookings and beat purchases completed by strangers.

**Actions:**
- Invite Alpha users individually via WhatsApp or DM. Personal invite only — no public announcement.
- Weekly feedback call (30 min) with a rotating group of 5-10 Alpha users. Record these calls.
- Launch the Discord server (private, invite-only at this stage).
- Begin posting TikTok content — at least 3 posts per week. Do not mention the platform name prominently yet; build the audience around the content category.
- Launch the email waitlist landing page publicly. The Alpha phase announcement is just the waitlist page going live.

**Exit Criteria:** At least 20 real studio bookings completed. NPS from Alpha users > 40. 500 waitlist signups.

---

### Phase 3 — Beta (Weeks 9-14)

**Who:** 500-1,000 waitlist members granted access in batches.

**Goal:** Prove the unit economics work at small scale. First revenue.

**Actions:**
- Open access in batches of 100 to manage support load and create scarcity perception ("batch 1 is open, join the waitlist for batch 2").
- Discord opens to all Beta users.
- Producer drop day: all 12 roster producers announce on their own channels simultaneously.
- Begin the YouTube/streamer outreach. Target confirmed coverage before Early Access.
- Run the first paid TikTok promotion — small budget (€200-500) to amplify the 1-2 best-performing organic videos.
- Launch the referral program (see `referral-program.md`).

**Exit Criteria:** €5,000 in revenue. At least 50 studio sessions booked. 1,500 waitlist signups. One external YouTube/streamer mention.

---

### Phase 4 — Early Access (Weeks 15-20)

**Who:** Full waitlist + public signup enabled.

**Goal:** Achieve repeatable growth. First users book their second session.

**Actions:**
- Remove the invite gate. Anyone can sign up. Waitlist members get a welcome email with a €10 first-booking discount.
- Submit to Product Hunt (see checklist below).
- A&R outreach begins in earnest (see `cold-email-templates.md`).
- Publish a case study: an artist who recorded at Neon Room, purchased a Metro Shadows beat, and released a track — showing the full Helicon workflow end-to-end.
- Begin Mix & Master add-on upsell campaign inside the booking confirmation flow.

**Exit Criteria:** €25,000 cumulative revenue. Week-over-week booking growth > 15%. Referral rate > 20% of new signups.

---

### Phase 5 — Full Launch (Week 21+)

**Who:** Public. PR push. Paid acquisition begins.

**Goal:** Scale what works. Reach Phase 1 of the revenue milestones.

**Actions:**
- Press outreach to Spanish music and tech media: Mondosonoro, Rolling Stone España, El Confidencial's music section.
- Expand studio partnerships beyond the initial 3 (Neon Room, The Vault, 808 Suite) to cover all of Madrid's key neighborhoods.
- Launch the Mix & Master add-on as a standalone product (not just a booking add-on).
- Begin paid Meta Ads targeting artists aged 18-28 in Madrid who follow Spanish trap artists.

## Product Hunt Launch Checklist

Product Hunt launches work when the community is primed. These steps assume a Tuesday or Wednesday launch (historically higher traffic days).

**6 weeks before:**
- [ ] Create Helicon's Product Hunt profile and verify the maker accounts of all team members.
- [ ] Identify 50 people (from the Discord, the waitlist, the Alpha/Beta community) who will upvote and leave genuine comments on launch day. Do not fake this — ask real users who had a real experience.
- [ ] Prepare the demo video: 90 seconds max. Show a real artist searching for a beat on Helicon, purchasing a Metro Shadows license, and booking a session at 808 Suite. End with the track playing.

**2 weeks before:**
- [ ] Write the Product Hunt tagline. Options to A/B test: "Beats + studios for independent artists in Madrid" vs "The infrastructure Spanish rap was missing."
- [ ] Prepare the first comment (from the founder) explaining the backstory — why Helicon exists, what problem they personally experienced.
- [ ] Schedule the launch for 00:01 PST on launch day (PH resets daily at midnight PST).

**Launch day:**
- [ ] Post in the Discord at 00:05 PST: "We're live on Product Hunt — support us here [link]."
- [ ] Email the waitlist at 9:00 AM Madrid time with the Product Hunt link.
- [ ] DM every Alpha and Beta user personally (not mass email — personal DM).
- [ ] Respond to every Product Hunt comment within 2 hours.
- [ ] Post TikTok and Instagram Stories showing the launch is live.

**Post-launch:**
- [ ] Write a "We launched on Product Hunt" post on Instagram with results and lessons. Even if the ranking is not #1, the content is valuable.

## Revenue Milestones — Road to €1M

### Phase 1 — €0 to €50,000

**Timeframe:** Months 1-4 (Internal through early Beta)

**Primary drivers:**
- Beat marketplace: 200 beat licenses sold at an average of €45 = €9,000
- Studio bookings: 400 hours booked at an average of €25/h = €10,000
- Mix & Master add-ons: 30 sessions at €150 = €4,500
- Custom beat commissions: 20 commissions at an average of €300 = €6,000

**What needs to be true:**
- At least 3 studios live on the platform with real-time availability.
- At least 50 beats listed from the 12 roster producers.
- The referral program is live and driving 20%+ of new user signups.
- CAC via organic/borrowed channels stays under €15.

**Weekly metrics to watch:**
- Waitlist signups (target: +50/week)
- Beat plays per week (proxy for intent)
- Studio bookings completed
- Revenue per booking session (tracks upsell attach rate)

---

### Phase 2 — €50,000 to €200,000

**Timeframe:** Months 5-10

**Primary drivers:**
- Beat marketplace volume scales with platform awareness. Target: 1,000 licenses/month at average €45 = €45,000/month run rate.
- Studio network expands to 8-10 studios. Booking volume: 1,500 hours/month at average €25/h.
- Producer affiliate program drives 30% of beat sales.
- Mix & Master attach rate improves to 25% of bookings.

**What needs to be true:**
- Repeat booking rate > 40% (artists return for a second session within 60 days).
- TikTok content is generating consistent inbound. At least one video per month with >100k views.
- A&R relationships established with 3-5 labels. Even if they are not paying customers yet, they are discovery partners.

**Weekly metrics to watch:**
- Repeat booking rate
- Beat license revenue by producer (identifies which producer to promote harder)
- Referral conversion rate (shares to signups to bookings)
- Mix & Master attach rate

---

### Phase 3 — €200,000 to €1,000,000

**Timeframe:** Months 11-24

**Primary drivers:**
- Geographic expansion: Barcelona, Valencia, Seville. Each new city adds a new studio cluster and a new producer cohort.
- Beat marketplace becomes a destination: 500+ beats, filterable by genre, mood, BPM. Monthly subscription tier introduced (e.g., €19/month for 2 licenses/month — targets prolific artists).
- Studio subscription: "Helicon Pro" — €99/month for 5 guaranteed hours, priority booking, 10% off additional time. Targets artists recording regularly.
- B2B channel: labels and management companies pay a monthly fee for bulk studio access and first-look on unreleased beats.

**What needs to be true:**
- LTV > €300 per artist (3+ bookings + 1+ beat purchase over 12 months).
- NPS > 50 consistently.
- The platform has enough social proof (known tracks made with Helicon beats/studios) to close skeptical artists.
- Paid acquisition (Meta, TikTok Ads) is profitable at CAC < €25 and LTV:CAC > 3:1.

**Weekly metrics to watch:**
- MRR and MRR growth rate
- LTV by acquisition channel
- Studio utilization rate per city (identifies where to add more supply)
- Producer earnings (if producers are earning well, they stay and recruit others)
